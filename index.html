Why this can work

Playbook for a fundable prediction product

1. Blend stable and volatile signals

Adjusted efficiency provides a stable base rate, while form and injury inputs let the model react to March-specific changes.

2. Make the model legible

Investors and users trust systems that explain picks with feature contributions, matchup leverage, and confidence intervals.

3. Optimize for decision quality

The winning consumer product is not “always right.” It helps users beat chalk, know when to zag, and manage bracket pool game theory.

Research notes

What informed the prototype

  • Four Factors: shooting, turnovers, rebounding, and free-throw rate.
  • Adjusted offensive and defensive efficiency remain core predictive signals.
  • Simulation beats single deterministic brackets for communicating uncertainty.
  • Perfect-bracket marketing is nonsense; expected value and calibration matter more.

Sources reviewed during design included NCAA.com bracket-odds explainers, Basketball- Reference’s Four Factors summary, and historical Kaggle March Madness modeling writeups.
